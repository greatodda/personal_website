    Here is a brief description of my research interests and projects

    I have participated in research across several disciplines during my undergraduate and graduate career. My freshman summer, I received the STAR scholarship to perform paid research with any Drexel faculty of choice. As a STAR Scholar, I did particle physics research under Dr. Russell Neilson where I set up from scratch, and performed GEANT4 simulations of the Drexel Bubble Chamber as part of the PICO collaboration which works to perform observational dark matter detection. I have also, as part of my coop experience, worked at the Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory (PPPL), a DOE national lab, with Dr. Igor Kaganovich and Dr. Stephane Ethier, where I delved in research in quantum chemistry and plasma physics. I worked on two projects during my time at PPPL - studying Boron Nitride Nanotube (BNNT) formation and studying etching of sillicon via fluorine. We published our results for the first project in 2021 (more information can be found in Publications section) and are correctly working on the manuscript for the second which should be published soon.
